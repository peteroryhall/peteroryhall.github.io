I like computational, structure-guided therapeutic design targeting membrane proteins and enjoy running, writing, and food.

Oxford

At the University of Oxford I work across the Departments of Physics and Biochemistry in the Tucker Lab. My work centres on the structural biology of K2P potassium channels, using cryo-EM to resolve how TASK-1 and TASK-3 channels gate, respond to pH, and are disrupted in disease. By capturing these channels in distinct functional states, I aim to establish a structural basis for selective therapeutic targeting.

Scripps Research

At Scripps Research I am based across the Ward and Forli Labs, where I bring computational approaches to bear on the same class of membrane protein targets. This arm of the work focuses on structure-guided drug discovery: using the structural information from Oxford alongside computational docking, design, and screening methods to identify and develop selective modulators.
